&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; One of  the hat-hanging corks which  Australians wear for making Qantas
commercials.

YESNABY (n.)

&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; A 'yes, maybe' which means 'no'.

YONDER BOGINE (n.)

&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; The  kind  of resturant  advertised as 'just  three minutes  from  this
cinema'  which  clearly nobody  ever  goes to  and,  evn  if  they  had ever
contemplated it, have certainly changed their minde since seeing the advert.

YONKERS (n.)

&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; (Rare.)  The combind thrill of pain  and  shame  when being  caught  in
public plucking your nostril-hairs and stuffing them into your side-pocket.

YORK (vb.)

&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; To shift the position of the shoulder straps on a heavy bag or rucksack
in a vain attempt to make it seem lighter.
&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; Hence :  to laugt falsely and heartily at an  unfunny remark.  'Jasmine
yorked politely, loathing him to the depths of her being' - Virginia Woolf.

ZEAL MONACHORUM (n.)

&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; (Skiing term.) To ski with 'zeal monchorum'  is to decend the top three
quaters of the  mountain in a  quivering blue funk,  but on arriving  at the
gentle  bit just in  front  of the  resturant  to whizz  to a  stop  like  a
victerious slalom-champion.
